## 1. Zen Planner — the template for a martial-arts vertical SaaS

**Business:** Founded 2006 by a martial arts school owner; acquired by Daxko. 6,000+ customers. Verticalized landing pages per niche (BJJ, MMA, martial arts school — no boxing-specific page).

**Pricing (site, 2026):** Studio from **$99/mo**, tiered by *active members* (a 150-student school ≈ $169/mo per their own blog). Add-ons: **Branded App $39/mo**, Website $99/mo, Engage (marketing automation) $249/mo, EMV hardware $39/mo. Payments via in-house Daxko Payments. Belt/skill tracking included in core.

**Revenue model:** SaaS base + add-on stack + payment processing (in-house processor = the real margin). Full feature access at every tier; price scales only with member count.

**Reported weaknesses ([Gymdesk review](https://gymdesk.com/blog/zen-planner-review), Capterra/GetApp):** perceived as expensive; high merchant processing fees; billing/cancellation complaints; persistent bugs and downtime reports. Owned by a portfolio company (Daxko) — innovation pace is a common gripe in reviews.

**OxRound implications:**
1. Zen Planner is proof the "martial-arts-native CRM" business works at $99–350/mo — and proof no one has claimed *boxing* specifically (their MMA/BJJ pages exist; boxing does not).
2. Their **$39/mo branded-app add-on** validates OxRound's website add-on pricing instinct ($49/mo) and suggests a branded/white-label app add-on is monetizable earlier than Phase 3.
3. Their FAQ sells **free onboarding + data migration** — OxRound's ONBOARDING.md playbooks are competitive necessities, not differentiators. Done-for-you onboarding at $299 must be *better*, not merely exist.
4. Belt tracking in core (not add-on) — OxRound's rank/progression tracking (Phase 3) is table stakes for the martial-arts segment; consider pulling a minimal version forward for boxing levels.

## 2. Mindbody — the incumbent whose unhappy customers are the market

**Business:** Market leader, no longer publishes pricing. Starter ≈ **$99–159/mo**, Accelerate ≈ **$259–279/mo** ([Koalendar analysis](https://koalendar.com/blog/mindbody-pricing-costs)). Consumer marketplace app is the differentiator — and the trap: **20% commission** on bookings that originate in the marketplace, plus **2.75–3.5% payment processing**.

**Reported weaknesses ([Vibefam Reddit roundup](https://vibefam.com/mindbody-reviews-reddit-2026/)):** gap between quoted price and actual spend (add-ons, marketplace commission, processing markup); support degradation (AI-routed, rotating reps, dedicated rep as paid add-on); operators stay despite dissatisfaction because switching is painful — lock-in, not loyalty.

**OxRound implications:**
1. The wedge is exactly what the investor brief says: **transparent flat pricing vs. Mindbody's fee stack.** Quantify it in sales collateral: a $10k/mo gym loses $275–350/mo to processing alone, before marketplace rake.
2. Support responsiveness is a purchasable advantage — MAINTENANCE.md's SLA table (24h/8h/2h) is a genuine sales weapon against a company whose #1 complaint theme is support.
3. Switching pain is the moat problem in reverse: OxRound's Type-D migration playbook (run 4 weeks parallel, white-glove) directly attacks Mindbody's retention mechanism.
4. Do NOT copy the marketplace model — its 20% rake is the most-hated feature. OxRound's "no per-booking fees, ever" can be a stated pricing principle.

## 3. Exercise.com — white-label as the entire business

**Business:** Sells a fully branded member app (gym's name/logo in the App Store) + platform. Pricing is quote-based, reported **from ~$125/mo (50-client cap)** up to custom enterprise; positioned for gyms doing ≥ ~$5k+/mo revenue ([iPT review](https://instituteofpersonaltrainers.com/encyclopedia-exercisecom-review), [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/152284/exercise-com/)). Reviews praise success-manager support; "price is a shock at first."

**OxRound implications:**
1. Validates that independent gyms pay a premium for **their own brand in the store**. OxRound's Growth tier ($299+, "white-label") has a proven buyer — but Expo makes per-gym branded builds operationally expensive (separate App Store listings, review cycles per gym). Price it accordingly (Exercise.com effectively charges $200+/mo for this) or defer it; do not bundle it cheaply.
2. Their quote-only pricing is a friction point OxRound exploits by publishing prices (the investor brief already commits to this: "owners should not need a finance degree").

## 4. Everfit — adjacent category, Phase-3 roadmap validation

**Business:** Coach→client training delivery (workout programming, nutrition, habits), 210k+ coaches claimed. Freemium: free to 5 clients, **Pro from $16–19/mo scaling to ~$242/mo at 300 clients**, Studio $88/mo, plus paid add-ons (meal plans $33–39, automation $24) ([QuickCoach pricing breakdown](https://www.quickcoach.fit/everfit-pricing-2026.html)). Heavy AI positioning (AI workout builder, MacroSnap). Revenue = per-client-count SaaS + add-on unbundling + marketplace (lead-gen, beta).

**OxRound implications:**
1. Not a CRM competitor — a boxing gym can't run billing/check-in/waivers on Everfit. But it is what OxRound's Phase 3 "training modules" will be compared against. Their add-on unbundling (charge separately for meal plans/automation) is a monetization pattern for OxRound's fight-camp/pad-work modules: **base CRM flat, boxing-depth modules as add-ons.**
2. Their per-client pricing creates bill anxiety as rosters grow — OxRound's flat per-location pricing is the counter-position, same as vs. Mindbody.
3. Coaches at OxRound gyms may already use Everfit/similar for programming. Phase 3 "workout plans" must either integrate or be genuinely boxing-specific (pad-work rounds, sparring load), not a worse generic clone.

## 5. GymWise.ai — the retention thesis, sold standalone

**Business:** Saudi pre-seed startup (Misk Launchpad, NTDP-backed; 9 gym branches, 10k members analyzed). Not a CRM — an AI layer that connects to existing gym software: churn prediction (claims 86% accuracy), behavioral habit-stage tracking, autonomous outreach including a multilingual **voice agent** that calls at-risk members. Pricing: book-a-call only.

**OxRound implications:**
1. Strongest possible validation of the logs.md July-2 note ("demonstrate how important user loss is with numbers"): an entire funded company exists to sell only the at-risk-member dashboard OxRound already built into the demo slice. Retention intelligence is a headline feature, not a reporting afterthought — lead demos with it.
2. Their architecture (layer on top, "nothing to migrate") is the inverse of OxRound's (own the system of record). Owning check-in data means OxRound's churn signals are first-party — a moat GymWise has to integrate to get.
3. Their autonomous voice/outreach agent previews where ROADMAP's Phase-3+ "autonomous lead conversion agent" market is heading; no action now.

## 6. Cross-cutting business findings

**Pricing position confirmed.** The market clusters at $99–160 entry / $250–350 full-stack. OxRound Starter $99–149 CAD / Pro $199 / Growth $299 sits correctly: cheaper than Mindbody+fees and Zen Planner+add-ons in like-for-like config, not so cheap it signals toy. Note CAD/USD: competitors price USD, so OxRound CAD pricing is effectively ~27% cheaper to Canadian gyms — worth saying explicitly in sales conversations.

**Everyone's real margin is payments.** Zen Planner (Daxko Payments in-house), Mindbody (2.75–3.5% + marketplace rake), Exercise.com (processing integrated). This is the strongest evidence yet for resolving **D-16**: gym-to-member payment processing (Phase 3, Stripe Connect + small transparent markup) is the industry-standard second revenue line — but the same evidence shows fee opacity is the #1 driver of churn/complaints. OxRound's version must be flat and published.

**Add-on architecture beats tier bloat.** Zen Planner (app $39, website $99, marketing $249) and Everfit (meal plans, automation) both monetize via unbundled add-ons on a modest base. Matches OxRound's planned add-ons (website widget $49, onboarding $299, payments %) — keep base plans simple, sell depth separately.

**The boxing gap is real.** Zen Planner serves MMA/BJJ/martial arts but has no boxing product line; nobody in this set does pad-work scheduling, fight-camp management, or boxing-specific member fields. The differentiation claim in the investor brief survives contact with the five competitors examined.

**Support is a feature.** Mindbody's most consistent complaint theme (support) and Exercise.com/Zen Planner's most consistent praise (support/success managers) point the same direction: at SMB gym scale, support quality decides renewals. MAINTENANCE.md SLAs should appear on the pricing page.

## 7. Actions for OxRound

1. Add "no per-booking fees, no marketplace commission, published pricing" as explicit positioning copy (anti-Mindbody).
2. Lead sales demos with the at-risk/revenue-at-risk dashboard (validated by GymWise's existence), then QR check-in.
3. Re-scope Growth tier white-label: either price it ≥$100/mo above Pro (Exercise.com evidence) or defer; Expo per-gym store listings are heavy ops.
4. Pull a minimal boxing level/rank tracker forward from Phase 3 into Phase 2 (Zen Planner ships belt tracking in core).
5. Resolve D-16 with a flat, published processing markup when Phase 3 arrives; never opaque.
6. Update ROADMAP competitor line: Everfit (Phase-3 training benchmark) and GymWise-style AI retention layers now tracked here.
